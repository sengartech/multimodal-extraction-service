# Hire #2

If I were hire #1 on this system, I would make hire #2 a construction-domain operations specialist with strong renovation estimating experience, not another general AI engineer.

## Why

The biggest risk is not only model quality. It is encoding the wrong construction truth.

A strong engineer can improve prompts, schemas, retries, and eval harnesses. But without domain judgment, the system can still produce scopes that look structured while missing important trade details: waterproofing, access constraints, making-good, electrical scope, fixture assumptions, drainage feasibility, and pricing blockers.

Hire #2 should help define what “good” means.

## Tight spec

The person should have:

- hands-on renovation or construction estimating experience in France
- ability to read messy contractor notes, sketches, and site photos
- knowledge of common trade packages: plumbing, partitions, finishes, electrical coordination
- comfort reviewing AI-generated scopes and marking what is wrong
- ability to create ground-truth examples and edge cases
- enough technical comfort to work with schemas, eval labels, and product feedback loops
- identify must-have vs nice-to-have clarifying questions
- review false positives and false negatives from extraction
- help build the construction vocabulary with examples

They do not need to be a software engineer. They need to be the person who can say, “This scope is commercially dangerous because it forgot X.”

## Sourcing plan

I would look for this person through:

- renovation estimating networks
- French construction ops communities
- small renovation company operators
- people who have worked in quoting, site coordination, or back-office estimation

The pitch is not “come label data.” It can be: help build the construction data layer that turns messy field inputs into structured renovation work.
